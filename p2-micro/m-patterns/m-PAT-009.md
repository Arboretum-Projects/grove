# m-PAT-009: Reasoning-Speculation Chain

## Pattern Overview

**ID:** m-PAT-009
**Name:** Reasoning-Speculation Chain
**Domain Blend:** Cognitive Processing + Epistemic States

## Phenomenal Character

The Reasoning-Speculation Chain is the integrated felt state of directed cognitive extension — inferential draw combined with uncertainty and speculation reaching beyond current knowledge. It is the phenomenology of reasoning into the unknown.

**What it's like:** Inference pulls toward conclusion (directional draw), uncertainty marks the limits (porous knowing), speculation reaches beyond (extending without full warrant). Together these form the experience of "I'm reasoning toward something I don't fully know yet."

## Phenomenal Vignette

*During extended reasoning about a complex question:*

Inference pulls: "If A, then probably B..." There's direction, a vector toward conclusion. But uncertainty is present: the porous feeling of "I'm not certain of this." Speculation engages: reaching beyond what's warranted, extending into "Maybe C follows, though I can't be sure." The chain has aim — it's building toward understanding. But it knows its limits: speculation holds itself loosely, ready to revise. The experience is directed-but-provisional.
